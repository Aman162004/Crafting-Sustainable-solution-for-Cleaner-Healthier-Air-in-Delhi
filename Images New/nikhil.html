Air Quality Index (AQI) Scale


Know about the category of air quality index (AQI) your ambient air falls in and what it implies.

Good (0 to 50)

The air is fresh and free from toxins. Enjoy outdoor activities without any health concerns.
Moderate (50 to 100)

Air quality is acceptable for most, but sensitive individuals might experience mild discomfort.
Poor (100 to 200)

Breathing may become slightly uncomfortable, especially for those with respiratory issues.
Unhealthy (200 to 300)

This air quality is particularly risky for children, pregnant women, and the elderly.
Limit outdoor activities.
Severe (300 to 400)

Prolonged exposure can cause chronic health issues or organ damage.
Avoid outdoor activities.
Hazardous (400 to 500+)

Dangerously high pollution levels.
Life-threatening health risks with prolonged exposure. Stay indoors and take precautions.

Prevent Health Problems: Understand Your Risks

Noida

Asthma

Risk of Asthma symptoms is High when AQI is Very Poor (200-300).

Severe symptoms, such as difficulty in breathing, chest tightness, and coughing.

Do's:

  • ✔ Use a mask while stepping outdoors.
  • ✔ Carry your inhaler at all times.
  • ✔ Check air quality daily and limit outdoor activities during high AQI.

Don'ts:

  • ✖ Engage in strenuous outdoor activities.
  • ✖ Ignore early signs of breathing discomfort.
  • ✖ Stay near industrial or construction zones.

Heart Issues

Risk of Heart Issues is High when AQI is Severe (300+).

Symptoms include chest pain, fatigue, and increased risk of heart attack.

Do's:

  • ✔ Stay in well-ventilated indoor spaces.
  • ✔ Keep prescribed medications handy.
  • ✔ Consult your doctor if symptoms worsen.

Don'ts:

  • ✖ Smoke or expose yourself to second-hand smoke.
  • ✖ Ignore warning signs like chest pain or dizziness.
  • ✖ Undertake physical exertion outdoors.

Allergies

Risk of Allergies is Moderate when AQI is Poor (50-200).

Common symptoms include sneezing, itchy eyes, and nasal congestion.

Do's:

  • ✔ Use air purifiers at home.
  • ✔ Wash your hands and face frequently.
  • ✔ Keep windows closed during high pollen or pollution levels.

Don'ts:

  • ✖ Avoid areas with high pollen or dust levels.
  • ✖ Touch your face or eyes without washing your hands.
  • ✖ Ignore persistent allergy symptoms.

Sinus

Risk of Sinus symptoms is Mid. when AQI is Poor (50-200).

Moderate to severe symptoms, such as a runny nose, facial pressure, and headache.

Do's:

  • ✔ Check air quality regularly to avoid exposure.
  • ✔ Avoid nasal allergy triggers.
  • ✔ Stay indoors during high pollution levels.

Don'ts:

  • ✖ Smoke or expose yourself to outdoor smoke.
  • ✖ Avoid strenuous activities outdoors.

Cold/Flu

Risk of Cold/Flu symptoms is High during seasonal changes and poor AQI.

Common symptoms include sore throat, fever, and body aches.

Do's:

  • ✔ Drink warm fluids to stay hydrated.
  • ✔ Wear warm clothes during cold weather.
  • ✔ Rest adequately to boost immunity.

Don'ts:

  • ✖ Expose yourself to sudden temperature changes.
  • ✖ Avoid overexertion or lack of sleep.
  • ✖ Neglect symptoms like high fever or prolonged cough.

Chronic (COPD)

Risk of COPD symptoms is Severe when AQI is Very Poor (200-300).

Severe symptoms, such as wheezing, breathlessness, and chronic cough.

Do's:

  • ✔ Use prescribed medications and inhalers regularly.
  • ✔ Stay in a pollution-free environment.
  • ✔ Consult a doctor if breathing issues worsen.

Don'ts:

  • ✖ Smoke or stay in smoky areas.
  • ✖ Ignore early symptoms of breathlessness.
  • ✖ Stay outdoors for long periods during high pollution.
